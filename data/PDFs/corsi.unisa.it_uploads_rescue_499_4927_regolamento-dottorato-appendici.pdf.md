ANNEX 2 – PhD PROGRAM ACTIVITIES 
 
Scientific Writing and Publishing 
Learning outcomes: To have a knowledge of the main types of technical and scientific documentation. To 
be able to conduct a bibliographic research. To be able to write a scientific article, a research proposal, a 
review, a technical presentation.  
 
Funding and management of research projects 
Learning outcomes: To have a knowledge about funding opportunities (where to find funding for your own 
research). To be able to write an effective research proposal for an international research call (how to get 
funding). To be able to manage research activities (you have been funded; and now?). 
 
Exploitation of research results 
Learning outcomes: To have a knowledge of the basic concepts regarding knowledge transfer. To be able to 
conduct contract and collaborative research. To be able to begin a patenting or licensing process. To be 
able to begin the process of creating a new company. 
 
Optimization Techniques for Engineers 
Learning outcomes: To be able to formulate real world optimization problems and incorporate uncertainty.  
To be able to implement optimization solvers. To be able to convert problems to their dual formulation. To 
be able to prove basic results on convex optimization. 
 
Computational paradigms for problem solving 
Learning outcomes : T o understand the main computationa l paradigms (a.k.a. computational styles): 
imperative, functional, logic, object-oriented, event-driven, concurrent. To be able to analyze and solve 
practical problems by recurring to the most suitable computational paradigm.  
 
English language 
Learning outcomes: To be able to understand a wide range of demanding, longer clauses, and recognize 
implicit meaning. To be able to express ideas fluently and spontaneously without much obvious searching for 
expressions. To be able to u se language flexibly and effectively for social, academic and professional 
purposes. To be able to produce clear, well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects, showing controlled 
use of organizational patterns, connectors and cohesive devices.  To reach the CEFR C1 level ( Effective 
Operational Proficiency, i.e. experienced enough to conduct PhD-related activities in English).  
 
Italian language 
Learning outcomes: To be able to understand the main points of clear standard input on familiar matters 
regularly encountered in work, school, leisure, etc. To be able to deal with most situations likely to arise while 
living in Italy. To be able to produce simple connected text on topics that are familiar or of personal interest. 
To be able to describe experiences and events. To reach the CEFR B1 level (Intermediate) at least. 
 
Independent learning 
Learning outcomes: To have a thorough knowledge of the literature and a comprehensive understanding of 
scientific methods and techniques applicable to his/her own research.

Supervised research activities, including attending international conferences and submitting scientific 
papers 
Learning outcomes: To be able to demonstrate originality in the application of knowledge, together with a 
practical understanding of how research are used to create and interpret knowledge in his/her field. To have 
developed the ability to critically evaluate current research and research techniques and methodologies. To 
have self-direction and originality in t ackling and solving problems. To be able to act autonomously in the 
planning and implementation of research. To have gained oral presentation and scientific writing skills.   To 
have effectively disseminate the results of the research activity. 
 
Research secondment period abroad 
Learning outcomes : In addition to those of supervised res earch activities, t o improve pr ofessional 
communication, presentation and interpersonal skills, networking skills in another research environment and 
social context. To develop resilience through coping with adversity or challenging circumstances. To increase 
awareness of and sensitivity to other cultural customs and norms. 
 
Drafting of the Doctorate Thesis 
Learning outcomes: To be able to correctly and effectively structure and present the research methodology 
carried on and the obtained results. To demonstrate knowledge of the  scientific state of the art. To show 
awareness of the relevance of theories and methods in relation to the research topic.
