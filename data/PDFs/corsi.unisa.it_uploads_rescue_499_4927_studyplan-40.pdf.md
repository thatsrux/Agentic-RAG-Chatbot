Study plan 
By completing an activity, students get awarded ECTS -credit points (in Italian, Credito Formativo 
Universitario - CFU). Every CFU represents the amount of accomplished workload: 1 CFU = 25 
hours; 1 year = 60 CFUs = 1500 hours. 
For the courses offered to PhD students there will be 6 hours of classes for each CFU, the other 19 
hours will be reserved to independent studies, homework, assigned projects 
 
The PhD program is divided into two curricula: 
(a) - Computer and Information Systems Engineering 
(b) - Information Technologies for Digital Medicine

Note (1): For students without a background in Machine Learning this course will be replaced by a Machine Learning 
course (9 CFU) of the Master Degree in Computer Engineering and the Elective Learning Activities will be limited to 6 
CFUs. For students in the curriculum Information Technologies for Digital Medicine, it is advised to choose the Master 
course on Embedded Systems for E-Health if the student does not have a sufficient background on embedded systems. 
Note (2): For foreign students from Countries where English is an official language the activity will be Italian language 
(level B1 CEFR at least). 
Note (3): All learning activities, including related assessments, have to be completed by the end of the second year.  
  

PhD program activities 
 
Advanced AI for Digital Medicine 
Learning outcomes : To acquire  knowledge and skills on the use of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning 
methodologies for the analysis and processing of large quantities of medical data (e.g. genomic data, medical images, 
collected data from wearable sensors) to support diagnosi s, therapeutic personalization and patient assistance, 
highlighting the specificities of the methodologies to be adopted in the domain of digital medicine (among these, the need 
for "explainability" of the response provided by the system, and the ability t o operate on data partially obfuscated for 
privacy reasons).  
 
Advanced machine learning 
Learning outcomes: To understand the theoretical foundations and the expected advantages (and disadvantages) of the 
state-of-the-art deep learning architectures. To understand the main limitations of traditional training processes, and the 
techniques proposed for improving the training effectiveness. To be able to choose or design an effective architecture for 
a complex problem; to design a learning system for processing non-vectorial data (sequences, trees or graphs); to exploit 
advanced techniques for accelerating the convergence of the training process for a complex architecture, or for transferring 
the knowledge learned in a different domain to a new problem. 
 
 
Computational paradigms for problem solving 
Learning outcomes : To understand the main computational paradigms (a.k.a. computational styles): imperative, 
functional, logic, object -oriented, event -driven, concurrent. To be able to analyze and solve practical problems by 
recurring to the most suitable computational paradigm. 
 
Drafting of the Doctorate Thesis 
Learning outcomes: To be able to correctly and effectively structure and present the research methodology carried on and 
the obtained results. To demonstrate knowledge of the scientific state of the art. To show awareness of the relevance of 
theories and methods in relation to the research topic. 
 
English language 
Learning outcomes: To be able to understand a wide range of demanding, longer clauses, and recognize implicit meaning. 
To be able to express ideas fluently and spontaneously without much obvious searching for expressions. To be able to 
use language flexibly and effectively  for social, academic and professional purposes. To be able to produce clear, well -
structured, detailed text on complex subjects, showing controlled use of organizational patterns, connectors and cohesive 
devices. To reach the CEFR C1 level (Effective Operational Proficiency, i.e. experienced enough to conduct PhD-related 
activities in English). 
 
Exploitation of research results 
Learning outcomes : To have a knowledge of the basic concepts regarding knowledge transfer. To be able to conduct 
contract and collaborative research. To be able to begin a patenting or licensing process. To be able to begin the process 
of creating a new company. 
 
Funding and management of research projects 
Learning outcomes: To have a knowledge about funding opportunities (where to find funding for your own research). To 
be able to write an effective research proposal for an international research call (how to get funding). To be able to manage 
research activities (you have been funded; and now?). 
 
Independent learning 
Learning outcomes : To have a thorough knowledge of the literature and a comprehensive understanding of scientific 
methods and techniques applicable to his/her own research. 
 
Innovation Management 
Learning outcomes: The course aims to provide students with the skills necessary to manage innovation processes within 
complex organizations. The course is structured to develop both technical skills and leadership skills, promoting an 
integrated approach to the management of in novation and the definition of business models in which, in addition to 
considering the technological aspects, the social, environmental and economic impacts are assessed , also taking into 
account the territorial sustainability of the proposed choices. 
 
Innovative research methodologies in industrial settings 

Learning outcomes: The course aims to provide an in-depth understanding of advanced research methodologies currently 
applied in state-of-the-art industrial contexts. Students will develop skills to propose and implement innovative research 
projects, using techniques and too ls to solve complex problems typical of industry. The course promotes collaboration 
between academia and industry to facilitate innovation. 
 
Italian language 
Learning outcomes : To be able to understand the main points of clear standard input on familiar matters regularly 
encountered in work, school, leisure, etc. To be able to deal with most situations likely to arise while living in Italy. To 
be able to produce simple connected tex t on topics that are familiar or of personal interest. To be able to describe 
experiences and events. To reach the CEFR B1 level (Intermediate) at least. 
 
Optimization Techniques for Engineers 
Learning outcomes: To be able to formulate real world optimization problems and incorporate uncertainty. To be able to 
implement optimization solvers. To be able to convert problems to their dual formulation. To be able to prove basic results 
on convex optimization. 
 
Research secondment period abroad 
Learning outcomes : In addition to those of supervised research activities, to improve professional communication, 
presentation and interpersonal skills, networking skills in another research environment and social context. To develop 
resilience through coping with adversity or challenging circumstances. To increase awareness of and sensitivity to other 
cultural customs and norms. 
 
 
Scientific Writing and Publishing 
Learning outcomes : To have a knowledge of the main types of technical and scientific documentation. To be able to 
conduct a bibliographic research. To be able to write a scientific article, a research proposal, a review, a technical 
presentation. 
 
Supervised research activities 
Learning outcomes : To be able to demonstrate originality in the application of knowledge, together with a  practical 
understanding of how research are used to create and interpret knowledge in his/her field. To have  developed the ability 
to critically evaluate current research and research techniques and methodologies. To  have self-direction and originality 
in tackling and solving problems. To be able to act autonomously in the  planning and implementation of research. To 
have gained oral presentation and sci entific writing skills. To  have effectively disseminate the results of the research 
activity.
