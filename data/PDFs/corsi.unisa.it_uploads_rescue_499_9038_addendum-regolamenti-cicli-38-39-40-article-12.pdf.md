Article 12 AUTHORSHIP AND AI TOOLS Students, as authors of their PhD thesis and authors/co-authors of papers, are fully responsible for the content of their manuscript, even those parts produced by an AI tool, and are thus liable for any breach of publication ethics. Moreover, as stated in The European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity, they, as researchers, must report their results and methods, including the use of external services or AI and automated tools, in a way that is compatible with the accepted norms of the discipline and facilitates verification or replication, where applicable. In particular, they are fully responsible for the use of outputs generated by AI tools and for the correctness of the processes in which these tools are employed. Responsibility cannot be transferred to the AI: those who use it can be held liable in the event of improper use, such as violations of scientific and editorial integrity standards (plagiarism, failure to cite sources, etc.). In compliance with the indications of the Committee of Publication Ethics (COPE), authors who use AI tools in the writing of a manuscript, production of images or graphical elements of the paper, or in the collection and analysis of data, must be transparent in disclosing in the Materials and Methods (or similar section) of the paper how the AI tool was used and which tool was used.
