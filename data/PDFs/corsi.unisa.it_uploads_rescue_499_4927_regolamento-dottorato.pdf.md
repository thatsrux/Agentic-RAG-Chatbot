PhD PROGRAM IN INFORMATION ENGINEERING 
RULES AND REGULATIONS 
 
Article 1 
SCOPE 
1. This document presents the rules and regulations for the Dottorato di Ricerca in Ingegneria 
dell’Informazione”, (PhD Program in Information Engineering), in compliance with article 16 of “Regolamento 
didattico di Ateneo” and with “Regolamento di Ateneo in materia di Dotto rato di Ricerca”, which rule and 
regulate any topic not considered here. 
2. The PhD Program in Information Engineering is promoted and organized by Dipartimento di Ingegneria 
dell’Informazione ed Elettrica e Matematica Applicata (DIEM). 
3. The PhD Program is managed by the Collegio dei Docenti (Board of Professors) chaired by the Coordinatore 
(Program Director). 
 
Article 2 
LEARNING OUTCOMES AND EXPECTED OCCUPATIONAL OUTPUTS 
The PhD Program in Information Engineering is addressed to qualified students who wish to acquire research 
and analytical skills at an internat ional level  about the generation, distribution, analysis, and use of 
information, data, and knowledge in systems. Information Engineering is used widely in all industries and 
comes from the merging of areas from computer, control, electronic and telecommun ications engineering. 
Main research areas concern the analysis, design, and performance optimization of computer and software 
systems, data analysis and knowledge engineering systems, telecommunication systems and networks, and 
control systems. 
The aim of the Program is to provide high -level training in the field of creation, management and 
dissemination of high -level scientific knowledge in the areas of interest. The objective is the creation of a 
high-level professional dedicated not only to research, but also to its management and to the development 
of innovation, in universities, research centers and companies. 
The PhD program therefore aims to train people able: 
• to analyze the state of the art and identify, propose, develop scientific and technological innovations; 
• to conduct methodological or experimental research; 
• to promote the acquisition and use of ICT technologies and methods in the services and production 
sectors, including through the creation of new companies; 
• to operate in research projects of a local, national and international nature in the context of 
collaborations with organizations and companies. 
A PhD is awarded in recognition of the fact that the person to whom it is awarded has: 
1. completed the PhD studies; 
2. demonstrated the ability to independently run a scientific project using scientific methods 
appropriate to the subject; 
3. helped to promote research at a level corresponding to the international standard for PhD programs 
within the discipline concerned; 
4. satisfactorily defended a doctoral thesis in a public viva voce examination. 
Professionals holding doctoral degrees have distinct adva ntages. If interested in becoming a researcher in 
Universities and Research Centers, a PhD is a requirement. Large companies often start those with PhD’s at 
higher levels immediately upon hiring, and it is becoming common for top ranking positions to be re served

Article 3 
ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS 
1. To be admitted to the PhD Program, a Master degree or other equivalent qualification obtained abroad 
and recognized as suitable is necessary. 
2. Access is through a competition for qualifications and exams, according to the procedures described in the 
“Regolamento di Ateneo in materia di Dottorato di Ricerca”. 
3. The Curriculum Vitae, the Master degree mark, any letters of presentation by schola rs from outside the 
University of origin, any publications, documentation of any other training or research activities carried out, 
constitute evaluable qualifications. The curriculum must include the list of exams taken during the Bachelor 
degree and Master degree, with the marks obtained and the main topics studied. 
4. During the exams, Candidates must demonstrate: knowledge of the main topics concerning Computer  
Engineering, Electronic  Engineering, Automation  Engineering and Telecommunications Engineerin g; 
analytical skills; knowledge of English at least at CEFR B2 level. Applicants may be asked to answer written 
questions. 
5. Each student admitted to the Course, taking into account the research projects underway at the 
Department and the student's skills, will be assigned a Tutor (Advisor), chosen from among the professors of 
the University, with the task of supervising student’s activities. 
6. Students are required to continuously interact with the Advisor to share the choice of the most suitable 
activities for the research project and to discuss the progress of the research in view of the final draft of the 
doctoral thesis. 
 
Article 4 
STRUCTURE OF THE PROGRAM 
1. The duration of the PhD Program is three years. 
2. PhD Program Activities are: 
• Compulsory courses offered to doctorate students. 
• Elective learning activities: Master degree courses taught at University of Salerno; other learning 
activities at a Master or Doctorate level providing certified accomplishment of the workload.  
• Independent learning (study of scientific and technical literature; mandatory attendance to seminars; 
support to teaching activities).

• Supervised research activities, including attending international conferences and submitting 
scientific papers. Activities may include stages in Italian companies or Institutions. 
• Research secondment period  abroad. 
• Drafting of the Doctorate Thesis. 
3. When completing an activity by obtaining a positive assessment on the final exam, students get awarded 
ECTS-credit points (in Italian, Credito Formativo Universitario - CFU). Every CFU represents the amount of 
accomplished workload: 1 CFU corresponds to 25 hours; 1 year corresponds to 60 CFUs or 1500 hours; the 
three years PhD Program corresponds to 180 CFUs. 
4. At the end of the first and second year of the PhD Program, students must submit a report of their activities, 
and present the results of their research activities to the Board of Professors. 
5. The Board evaluates the independent learning and the research  activities and assigns a mark. Possible 
grades are, in descending order: ottimo  (excellent), buono  (good), discrete ( fair), su fficiente (pass), 
insufficiente (fail). Students who obtain an insufficiente (fail) mark are excluded from the PhD Program and 
lose their scholarships – if any.

Article 7 
ATTENDANCE OBLIGATIONS 
1. The PhD study and research work is carried out, full time, during the three years of the PhD Program. 
2. Students with scholarship can apply to be authorized for occasional and time-limited working activities. 
These activities must be aimed at acquiring skills related to the PhD Program and must in no case affect the 
PhD Program activities.  In case the activities are authorized  by the Board of Professors , the Board  may 
request the student to present additional reports to check if the working activities are actually not affecting 
PhD Program activities. 
3. Students without scholarship can apply to be authorized to carry out work activities by demonstrating that 
they are compatible with the PhD program activities . In the event that  the student is an employee, a 
declaration by the employer is also required stating awareness of the student’s status and commitment to 
grant work leaves to take part in the compulsory activities of the  PhD Program. In case the activities are 
authorized by the Board of Professors, the Board may request the student to present additional reports to 
check if the working activities are actually not affecting PhD Program activities.

Article 8 
EXAMS 
1. For the compulsory courses offered to PhD students, achievement of the learning outcome will be assessed 
by a Commission appointed by the Board of Professors  which assigns a mark . Possible grades are, in 
descending order: ottimo  (excellent), buono  (good), discrete ( fair), sufficiente  (pass), insufficiente  (fail). 
Students who get an insufficiente (fail) mark or reject the given mark can repeat the exam only once after at 
least 30 days. 
2. For other courses that certify CFUs, the marks obtained will be converted by the Board of Professors to 
the same scale. Grades e xpressed in 30ths are converted as: 2 9-30 e lode ottimo (excellent), 26-28 buono 
(good), 23-25 discreto (fair), 18-22 sufficiente (pass), 0-17 insufficiente (fail). 
  
Article 9 
FINAL EXAM 
1. The main objective of the PhD career is the development of an original research contribution. The PhD 
thesis is expected to contribute to the advancement of the knowledge in the candidate's research field.  
2. At the conclusion of the PhD studies, students must submit a report on their activities and a draft of the 
thesis, and present the results of their research activities to the Board of Professors. 
2. T he Board evaluates the whole of student’s activities  and gives a final mark. Possible grades are, in 
descending order: ottimo (excellent), buono (good), discrete (fair), sufficiente (pass), insufficiente (fail). 
3. Students who receive a positive assessment can submit their theses to two external reviewers, chosen by 
the Board , for refereeing. If the assessment provided by the reviewers is positive (or after the revisions 
possibly required by them), students can defend their thesis in a final exam, before a Committee. 
4. After the thesis defense,  the Committee  assigns the PhD degree with a mark. Possible grades are: 
eccellente con lode (excellent cum laude), ottimo (excellent), buono (good), discrete (fair), sufficiente (pass).  
The mark is given depending on the presentation and defense of the Thesis, the quality of the obtained 
research results, the level of the publications, the marks obtained by the student during his/her career. The 
excellent cum laude grade may be assigned only unanimously in case of outstanding research results and has 
to be adequately motivated by the Committee. 
 
Article 10 
DOUBLE-TITLE PROGRAM FOR STUDENTS OF THE UNIVERSITY OF SALERNO. 
1. Students in their first year , in agreement with their Advisors , may apply for a double -title program in 
cooperation with a Foreign University with a PhD Program related to Information Engineering. 
2. In case there is not already an agreement with the Foreign University, t he double-title program will be 
activated only if an agreement can be reached. Agreements must contain the following rules. 
3. The double-title program has a duration of four years (240 CFUs) , that is one year more than the regular 
program. The additional year will not be funded by the University of Salerno scholarship, if any. The Foreign 
University will be required to fund, as a minimum, the additional year. 
4. In addition to the study plan in Annex 1: there must be at least 15 CFUs of learning activities in the Foreign 
University. The duration of the activity in the Foreign University must be between 18 and 24 months (from 
90 to 120 CFUs, including the 15 for learning activities). 
5. Students in the double-title program will also be enrolled at the Foreign University and will receive both 
PhD titles after the final exams.

Article 11 
DOUBLE-TITLE PROGRAM FOR STUDENTS OF FOREIGN UNIVERSITIES. 
1. Students from a Foreign University enrolled in a  PhD Program related to Information Engineering may 
apply for a double-title program in cooperation with the University of Salerno. 
2. In case there is not already an agreement with the Foreign University, the double -title program will be 
activated only if an agreement can be reached. Agreements must contain the following rules. 
3. T he Bo ard of Professors authorize s the  enrollment at the University of Salerno depending on the 
qualification of the applying students. 
4. The double-title program has a duration of four years (240 CFUs).  
5. Students must spend a period between 18 and 24 months (from 90 to 120 CFUs) at the University of 
Salerno. During this period students must acquire at least 15 CFUs of learning activities. 
6. Students in the double-title program will also be enrolled at the University of Salerno and will receive both 
PhD titles after the final exams. 
7. In the agreement, the case of students from University of Salerno applying for a double-title program must 
also be considered (see Article 10)
