University of Salerno 
PhD Program in Information Engineering 
 
Minimal requirements to be met by Students by the end of each year 
 
1st Year 
By the end of the 1 st year of the PhD Program, you should have a thorough knowledge of the relevant 
literature in your research field and should be able to make an appreciation of the key material in it. Also, you 
should have identified a research objective and a credible methodology to reach it. 
 
It will be required to you to present a report on: 
• Background (20-25 pages) 
o basic facts (existing knowledge) 
o importance of the field 
o literature review (around 15-20 pages - this should be particularly taken care of as it would 
be a draft for one of the chapters of your final thesis ; all references should be properly 
included and provided in full, correct format at the end of the report) 
• Justification for the proposed research and research plan (5 pages) 
o identifying a research 'gap' 
o aims/hypotheses/questions 
o putting the proposed research in context of the published literature (how your work fits into 
the bigger picture) 
o methodology (describe the methods and the techniques to be applied and how progress will 
be assessed, e.g. simulation and/or laboratory experiments and/or field trials, etc.) 
o timing  (appreciated but not compulsory) 
o value of the research (show where you expect the research to lead in the next 2 years, usually 
linked to the identified gap above) 
• Preliminary results, if any (3-5 pages - this section is not compulsory for the first year) 
o describe the main results already obtained and problems encountered 
o link them to the overall research plan 
o list the submitted/presented/published papers and/or internal reports that fully describe 
the results and attach them 
• Other activities you have performed 
o External collaborations ( if there have been collaborations with othe r research groups, list 
them and describe what have been done)  
o Activities outside the University of Salerno (Where? When? What has been the purpose?)  
o Conference attendance (Where? When? What has been the purpose?) 
o Involvement in Research Projects ( Have you been officially involved in national and 
international projects?) 
o Learning activities (taking into account the planned learning activities, list the accomplished 
ones - attach an S3 certificate) 
o Support to teaching activities ( Have you supported t eaching? What courses? How? Do not 
include support you have been paid for) 
o Other activities (e.g., papers reviewing, conference committees, etc.) 
 
The 1st  year report is designed to help you focus on where your work is leading and put it in context, as well 
as practice preparing a substantial scientific report: some of the material may well form the basis of the 
introductory chapters in the final thesis or serve as a useful reference during the remainder of the program. 
 
A three -member committee for each student will be appointed by the Board of Professors (the student’s 
advisor cannot be a member; one of the member is the PhD Program Director, if not the student’s advisor).  
The committee will read your report, interview you, (ask for your Advisor’s opinion, if needed) , and evaluate 
your performance during the 1st year, making a motivated proposal for the marks of the Independent Learning 
and Supervised Research activities.  
You will then make an oral presentatio n of your activities to the Board of Professors which will assign the 
final marks for these activities. 
The motivation of the marks will be provided to you as a feedback.

2nd Year 
By the end of the 2nd year of the PhD Program, you should have actively pursued your research objectives and 
you should have got preliminary results that confirm (or confute) that the chosen methodology is effective 
to reach the objectives. If there has been a confutation, a  full update of the research plan should be 
submitted. 
 
It will be required to you to present a report on: 
• Background (20-25 pages) 
o Update of the 1st year draft 
• Update of the research plan (5-7 pages) 
o carefully justify any proposed modification 
o be sure to clarify the expected original contribution with respect to the state of the art  
• Preliminary results, if any  (results of the first two years, 5-7 pages) 
o describe the main results already obtained 
o link them to the overall research plan 
o list the submitted /presented/published papers and/or internal reports that fully describe 
the results and attach them 
• Other activities you have performed (first two years) 
o External collaborations ( if there have been collaborations with other research groups, list 
them and describe what have been done)  
o Activities outside the University of Salerno (Where? When? What has been the purpose?)  
o Conference attendance (Where? When? What has been the purpose?) 
o Involvement in Research Projects ( Have you been officially involved i n national and 
international projects?) 
o Learning activities (taking into account the planned learning activities, list the accomplished 
ones - attach a S3 certificate) 
o Support to teaching activities ( Have you supported teaching? What courses? How? Do not 
include support you have been paid for) 
o Other activities (e.g., papers reviewing, conference committees, etc.) 
 
Assessment of student’s activities will be carried out  as in the 1st year, 
 
 
3rd Year  
By the end of the 3 rd year you should have got a good competence in your research area, have achieved (at 
least part of) your research objectives, and have results that confirm that you have been able to advance the 
state of the art. Hopefully, your research results should have been confirmed by peer-review as well, at least 
in part. You should have written an advanced draft of your PhD thesis.  
 
It will be required to you to present a report on: 
• Extended abstract of your thesis (5-7 pages) 
• Other activities you have performed (all three years) 
o External collaborations ( if there have been collaborations with other research groups, list 
them and describe what have been done)  
o Activities outside the University of Salerno (Where? When? What has been the purpose?)  
o Conference attendance (Where? When? What has been the purpose?) 
o Involvement in Research Projects ( Have you been officially involved in na tional and 
international projects?) 
o Learning activities (taking into account the planned learning activities, list the accomplished 
ones - attach a S3 certificate) 
o Support to teaching activities ( Have you supported teaching? What courses? How? Do not 
include support you have been paid for) 
o Other activities (e.g., papers reviewing, conference committees, etc.) 
 
and to submit an advanced draft of your PhD thesis to the Board of the Professors.  
 
Assessment of student’s activities will be carried out  as in the 1st year.
