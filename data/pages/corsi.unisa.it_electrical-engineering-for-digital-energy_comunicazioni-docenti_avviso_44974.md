#  Electrical Engineering for Digital Energy | 27 marzo 2025 - Seminario - FLORIN “FLuorescent nanO-agents for super-Resolution Imaging and seNsing” - Horizon Europe project (GA 101086142)
## Electrical Engineering for Digital Energy 27 marzo 2025 - Seminario - FLORIN “FLuorescent nanO-agents for super-Resolution Imaging and seNsing” - Horizon Europe project (GA 101086142)
Gentili studenti, la presente per invitarvi al seminario "**Spectroscopic manifestation of a weak van der Waals interaction between trans-stilbene and hexagonal boron nitride surface** " by Ivan HALIMSKI*, PhD at FTMC**, Vilnius (LT) che si terrà giovedì 27 marzo 2025 alle ore 15 presso l'aula P16 (building F3) e per il quale è prevista anche la partecipazione da remoto.
A valle del seminario, Ivan Halimski presenterà anche la sua esperienza nell'ambito dei progetti di ricerca in cui è coinvolto, con un contributo dal titolo "Research perspectives in implementing international projects", aprendo sullo scenario delle possibili strade che si aprono dopo la laurea, con l'intenzione di ispirare le vostre porospettive future.
Di seguito il programma
15:00 **Opening on Horizon Europe MSCA-Staff Exchange project FLORIN - GA 101086142** – prof. Patrizia LAMBERTI, Principal Investigator for University of Salerno and prof. Paolo CIAMBELLI, CEO of Narrando company
15:10 **Spectroscopic manifestation of a weak van der Waals interaction between trans-stilbene and hexagonal boron nitride surface** – Ivan Halemski, PhD student and Early Stage Research at Center for Physical Sciences and Technology (FTMC), Vilnius - Lithuania (secondee at Narrando, ITALY, within FLORIN project)
15:30 **Discussion - "Research perspectives in implementing international projects" -** by Ivan Halemski
15:45 **Closing**
[*] Ivan Halimski is PhD student and Early Stage Researcher from FTMC (Fizinių ir Technologijos Mokslų Centras - Center for Physical Sciences and Technology) of Vilnius (LITHUANIA). He is in “secondment” in Italy to NARRANDO company (innovative start-up and former spin-off of the University of Salerno) within the Horizon Europe Marie Curies Staff Exchange project FLORIN
[**] CENTER FOR PHYSICAL SCIENCES AND TECHNOLOGY (FTMC) is the largest scientific research institution carrying out a unique fundamental research and technological development works in scientific fields of laser technologies, optoelectronics, nuclear physics, organic chemistry, bio and nanotechnologies, electrochemical material science, functional materials, electronics, in Lithuania. FTMC was established in 2010 by joining institutes of Chemistry, Physics, Semiconductor Physics in Vilnius and Textile institute in Kaunas. Over the recent years the Center combining different science branches has become one of the leading scientific institutions in Lithuania.
[***] For information and link, please contact plamberti@unisa.it
